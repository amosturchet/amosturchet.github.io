Algebraic Geometry and Number Theory Seminars

Junior Prof. Dr. Ariyan Javanpeykar

Johannes Gutenberg-Universität Mainz

Arakelov invariants, Belyi's theorem, and Szpiro's small points conjecture


April 4th - 14th 2016

Description:

During April 2016, Ariyan Javanpeykar (Johannes Gutenberg-Universität Mainz) will give a series of lectures on the relationship between Arakelov invariants, Belyi's theorem and Szpiro's small points conjecture.

Abstract

A Belyi map on a complex algebraic curve is a rational function with at most three critical points. In this series of lectures we first explain Belyi's theorem: a complex algebraic curve can be defined over a number field if and only if has a rational function with at most three critical points.

Belyi's theorem allows one to attach a natural invariant \(d(X)\) to every curve \(X\) defined over a number field. Indeed, we define the Belyi degree \(d(X)\) of \(X\) defined over a number field to be the minimal degree of a Belyi map on \(X\). The first lecture will be concerned with basic properties of the Belyi degree and basic examples (e.g. the Belyi degree of the Fermat and modular curves).

Next, we move our attention to Arakelov theory of curves \(X\) over number fields. Arakelov and Faltings defined a pairing on the set of closed points of such a curve with properties analogous to the intersection pairing on a smooth projective complex algebraic surface (e.g. Noether's formula, Riemann-Roch, and the Hodge index theorem). This pairing allows one to define a canonical height function on the set of points of \(X\).

The first result we discuss provides a precise relation between Arakelov theory and Belyi's theorem. Indeed, we will show that on each curve there exist infinitely many points whose height is bounded by a polynomial in \(d(X)\). We will use this to give a new approach to Szpiro's small points conjecture.

Before we move our attention to Szpiro's small points conjecture, we note that Arakelov theory also naturally provides us with several intrinsic invariants of a curve defined over a number field. One of these natural invariants is the Faltings height. We will use Faltings's Hodge index theorem and the existence of a point with height bounded by a polynomial in the Belyi degree to obtain an explicit polynomial bound for the Faltings height of a curve in terms of the Belyi degree. Applying this to modular curves shows that the Couveignes-Edixhoven-Bruin algorithm for computing coefficients of modular forms runs in polynomial time, under GRH. Applying this to an arbitrary cover of curves resolves a conjecture of Edixhoven-de Jong-Schepers.

We conclude this series of lectures by explaining our joint work with Rafael von Kaenel on Szpiro's small points conjecture for cyclic curves of prime degree over number fields. This will require us discussing the theory of minimal regular models of such curves over rings of integers of number fields and height bounds on the set of solutions to the unit equation in a given number ring.
