Marco Maculan

GIT and Diophantine Approximation


August 24th - September 4th 2015

Description:

During August and September 2015, Marco Maculan (Institut Mathématiques de Jussieu - Paris VI) will give a series of lectures on Geometric Invariant Theory and Diophantine Approximation. The lectures will begin with a basic introduction to Geometric Invariant Theory (GIT) - a theory developed by Mumford in the '60s to construct moduli spaces - and then will expound a proof of Roth's Theorem - a classical result in Diophantine Approximation - based on techniques of GIT.

List of topics covered:

  1. GIT I : Quotients of affine algebraic varieties
  2. GIT II : Semi-stable points and quotients of projective varieties
  3. Complex and p-adic Kempf-Ness theory
  4. Roth's Theorem I : Statement and classical proof
  5. Roth's Theorem II : proof of Dyson's Lemma (and a sketch of proof in higher dimension)
  6. Proof of Roth's Theorem through GIT
