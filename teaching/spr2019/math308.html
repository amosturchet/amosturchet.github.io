Announcements and Schedule:

Week 10
Diagonalization and applications: Google Page Rank. Review.

Week 9
Diagonalization: definition, properties and examples.

Week 8
Midterm 2. Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors: motivation, properties and examples.

Week 7
Change of Basis: examples, definitions and properties. Determinants: properties, examples and computations.

Week 6
Basis: definition, properties and computations. Column Space, Row Space, Rank and Nullity of a matrix. The rank-nullity Theorem.

Week 5
Midterm 1. Subspaces: definition, properties, kernel of a linear transformation, null space of a matrix.

Week 4
Matrix Multiplication. Composition of linear transformations. Inverses.

Week 3
Linear Independence: definition and properties. Linear Transformations: definition, examples. Domain, Codomain, Range, 1-to-1 and onto.

Week 2
Vector form of linear systems. Span: definition, properties and relation to linear systems.

Week 1
Linear Systems: triangular systems and systems in echelon form. Augmented matrix, Gauss-Jordan Algorithm.

Course Description:

Math 308 is an introductory course in linear algebra intended for students in engineering, mathematics, and the sciences. Topics will include systems of linear equations, vector spaces, matrices, subspaces, orthogonality, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and applications.

Textbook:

  • Main Textbook
    Linear Algebra with Applications by Jeffrey Holt. You may purchase a hard copy of the text at the bookstore or use the electronic version that comes with WebAssign.
  • Further Readings
    These YouTube videos by 3Blue1Brown are an excellent resource for understanding the geometry behind many of the concepts we will learn in this class.
