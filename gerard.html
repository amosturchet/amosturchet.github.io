Algebraic Geometry and Number Theory Seminars

Gerard Freixas i Montplet

CNRS, Institut Mathématiques de Jussieu - Paris VI

An introduction to Arakelov geometry


November 17th - 26th 2015

Description:

During November 2015, Gerard Freixas (CNRS, Institut Mathématiques de Jussieu - Paris VI) will give a series of lectures on Arakelov Geometry.

Abstract

The aim of these series of talks is to present some basics on Arakelov geometry on arithmetic surfaces. I will start by reformulating Minkowski's geometry of numbers in a geometric language, and recall some applications like the finitenness of the class numbers. Then I will switch to arithmetic surfaces, where we will study the arithmetic intersection pairing and the statement of the arithmetic Riemann-Roch formula. I will discuss some examples to clarify the kind of information this statement is about, for instance the case of the projective line and elliptic curves with complex multiplication and "hyperbolic" curves. Depending on time, I will discuss an asymptotic version known as the arithmetic Hilbert-Samuel theorem, that will bring us back to Minkowski's theory.
